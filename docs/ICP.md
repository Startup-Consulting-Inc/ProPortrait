# ProPortrait AI — Ideal Customer Profiles

> **Version**: 1.0 — March 2026
> **Product**: ProPortrait AI (full feature set — identity locks, 16 styles, 5 expressions, editing studio, platform exports, Pro tier)

---

## How These Two Were Selected

The full ICP space for ProPortrait spans seven use cases: job seekers, freelancers/creatives, executive leadership, dating profiles, academics, tech professionals, and remote teams. The two profiles below were chosen because:

1. They are maximally distinct from each other in psychology, trigger events, and feature usage — no meaningful overlap.
2. Both have high purchase intent — the trigger event is acute and concrete, not vague.
3. Both have the highest lifetime value — repeat use and Pro upgrade likelihood are both strong.
4. Together they cover the two dominant willingness-to-pay postures: urgency-driven single-session purchase and identity-driven recurring use.

---

## ICP 1 — "The Career Pivotter"

> **"I need to look like the person I'm becoming, not who I was."**

### Demographics

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| Role | Mid-career professional in transition — Senior Analyst → Product Manager, Engineer → Engineering Manager, Consultant going independent, Director going fractional |
| Industry | Technology, consulting, financial services, healthcare administration |
| Age | 28–42 |
| Income | $85K–$160K current; targeting $110K–$200K next role |
| Location | Urban and tier-1 suburban — NYC, SF Bay Area, Seattle, Austin, Chicago, Boston, Toronto, London |
| Status at trigger | Just laid off (3–8 weeks in), actively interviewing while employed, or recently promoted with an outdated photo |

### Psychographics

**Personality**: High agency, research-before-buying, checklist mentality. They treat job searching as a project. Not vain about appearance but highly strategic about professional optics.

**Values**: Meritocracy — they resent that presentation matters but will invest strategically when ROI is clear. Authenticity is important: they want to look like a better version of themselves, not a stock photo person.

**Self-image**: Competent and underrepresented by their current digital presence. Their LinkedIn photo is 4–7 years old and no longer matches the seniority level they are targeting.

### Current Situation

Their profile photo is visibly dated. Common scenarios:
- Photo from 2018–2021; hair, weight, or age has visibly changed
- Photo cropped from a company event — partial colleague visible, or a hotel ballroom background
- Title recently changed from IC to manager but the photo still reads "mid-level contributor"
- Mismatch across platforms: professional-ish LinkedIn, casual selfie on GitHub, nothing on their personal site

They have considered a photographer but the $300–$800 session cost and 2–4 week scheduling lead time conflict with the urgency of an active search.

### Primary Pain Points

1. **The dated photo problem** — Recruiters see the photo before anything else. An outdated photo signals a level below their target. This is understood as a competitive disadvantage, not vanity.
2. **Identity drift anxiety** — They tried (or know someone who tried) a cheap AI headshot tool. The result had different eye color, wrong skin tone, completely changed hair. They have lost trust in AI headshots as a category. ProPortrait's identity locks are the specific feature that overcomes this objection.
3. **Multi-platform coordination** — They need photos at different specs for LinkedIn, their resume PDF, their personal website, and GitHub. Four different photos at four different quality levels is a frustration they want solved in one session.
4. **Speed incompatibility with active search** — A recruiter screen may be in 48 hours. A photographer is not an option on that timeline.
5. **Cost sensitivity when between jobs** — They can justify $9.99 effortlessly. They cannot justify $500 for a photo session while unemployed.

### Trigger Events

| Trigger | Description |
|---|---|
| Layoff + 2 weeks | Initial networking adrenaline fades; they audit their profile and cringe at the photo |
| Recruiter outreach received | They click their own LinkedIn to see what the recruiter sees. Photo problem becomes undeniable. |
| First interview scheduled | They Google themselves to prep. The visual mismatch hits. |
| Promotion finalized | Title changes from IC → manager. Old photo is now formally incongruent. |
| "Increase your visibility" directive | Manager tells them to start speaking or writing. Their photo will be attached to every bio. |

### Feature Affinity

| Feature | Priority | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| LinkedIn style | Critical | Primary output. Needs to look authentic on LinkedIn without looking AI-generated. |
| Resume style | High | 3:4 portrait needed for PDF resumes and application portals that require a photo. |
| Platform batch export (Download All) | High | One action produces LinkedIn 800×800 + Resume 600×800 simultaneously. |
| Identity locks (eye color, skin tone, hair length) | Critical | The specific feature that overcomes prior AI headshot trauma. |
| Confident Neutral expression | High | Does not want a forced smile. Wants to look competent, not eager — right for senior-level presence. |
| Before/after comparison slider | Medium | Validates they still look like themselves after generation. Reduces identity drift anxiety. |
| Likeness strength (default 70) | Medium | Will leave at default or push to 80. |
| Corporate style | Secondary | Fallback if LinkedIn feels too informal for their industry (finance, law). |
| 2048px export (Pro) | Medium | For resume PDF and personal website hero. |
| Transparent background (Pro) | Low–Medium | Relevant if they have a personal site with a non-white background. |

**Not relevant**: Dating, Cartoon, Art Deco, Vintage, Cinematic styles; regional editing (clothes/background) — they will accept whatever the AI generates if it looks professional.

### Willingness to Pay

**Free tier**: They try free first. Produce a JPG at 1024px. Decision time under 10 minutes.

**Pro upgrade trigger**: Free result is good enough that they want it for their resume PDF (needs 2048px) or their personal site (transparent background). At $9.99, for someone targeting a $130K role, this is not a decision — it is a rounding error. The barrier is trust, not price.

**Estimated Pro conversion**: 35–50% of users who reach Step 4.

**Repeat use**: Low per session, but returns every 12–24 months (next job transition, speaking opportunity, major title change). Strong referrals within job-search cohorts and peer networks.

### Discovery Channels

1. Reddit — r/cscareerquestions, r/jobs, r/ITCareerQuestions ("cheap/fast professional headshot" threads)
2. Google — "AI professional headshot," "LinkedIn profile photo AI," "cheap professional headshot"
3. LinkedIn peer observation — sees a connection update their profile photo, it looks suspiciously good, asks how
4. Product Hunt — browses when building out their job-search tool stack
5. Twitter/X — career advice accounts sharing "how I updated my LinkedIn for free"

### Objections

| Objection | Response |
|---|---|
| "It won't look like me." | Identity lock UI + before/after comparison slider. Surface locks prominently before generation. |
| "Recruiters will know it's AI." | Naturalness slider + Polished preset avoids over-smoothed uncanny valley. |
| "I need a specific environment / company colors." | Regional background editing. Needs to be surfaced — they may not know it exists. |
| "I should just book a photographer." | Speed and cost. If the interview is in 48 hours, the photographer is not an option. |
| "What does Google do with my photo?" | Privacy notice — "no server-side storage" — must be visible before upload, not buried. |

### Success Metrics

They consider ProPortrait successful if:
- 3+ people in their network notice the new photo without an announcement
- LinkedIn profile views increase in the week after updating
- The photo passes the "do I still look like myself" test with a trusted friend
- They feel confident using the same photo in a Zoom interview thumbnail and on their resume

## ICP 2 — "The Fractional Expert"

> **"My face is part of my product. It needs to work across every surface I show up on."**

### Demographics

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| Role | Freelancer, independent consultant, fractional executive, solo founder, creator-adjacent professional — Fractional CMO, Independent UX Consultant, Brand Strategist, Executive Coach, Course Creator, LinkedIn Creator, Podcast Host, Developer Advocate |
| Industry | Creator economy, consulting, B2B SaaS (vendor side), professional coaching, design, content marketing, edtech |
| Age | 30–50 |
| Income | $80K–$300K+; variable and project-dependent |
| Location | Remote-first; often former major metro residents now in secondary cities; strong in Southeast US, Midwest, European remote hubs |
| Status | Self-employed; no HR department, no budget approval process, no scheduling dependencies |

### Psychographics

**Personality**: High autonomy, brand-conscious, systems thinker. They think of themselves as a media company of one. They understand that their photo is a brand asset, not an ID card. They have strong opinions about how they want to come across and will not accept a generic output.

**Values**: Authenticity with polish. They want to look like the best version of themselves. They actively resist anything that looks templated or corporate — they have escaped that world and their photo must not suggest otherwise. Time is directly billable; they value efficiency at an operational level.

**Self-image**: A trusted expert in their domain actively building a recognized personal brand. Consistency across touchpoints — website, newsletter, course platform, conference bio, Substack, Twitter, LinkedIn — is something they think about deliberately.

### Current Situation

They have a better-than-average photo by general standards, but it is not working across their full surface area. Common scenarios:
- Great corporate headshot from 2 years ago — looks professional but signals "employee," not "independent expert"
- Newsletter, podcast, and consulting practice each have a slightly different photo from a different era
- Speaking opportunity secured; event organizer needs a high-impact "speaker photo" — current photos are the wrong style or wrong resolution
- Brand aesthetic has evolved (moved from corporate blue to warmer personal look); existing photos no longer match
- They have used a photographer before but the results felt stiff and the turnaround was 2+ weeks

### Primary Pain Points

1. **The multi-surface consistency problem** — Their face appears in 6–10 different contexts (website hero, newsletter header, LinkedIn, Twitter, conference bio, course platform, podcast cover art, Substack, speaking reel). Managing consistent, current, high-quality photos across all of these is an ongoing operational problem, not a one-time fix.
2. **Style does not match positioning** — A corporate headshot signals "I work for someone." A casual shot signals "I'm not serious." They need something that says "independent authority" — a specific aesthetic that most photographers do not default to.
3. **Speed to market for launches** — When they launch a new offer, they want a simultaneous visual refresh. Waiting 2–3 weeks for a photographer is incompatible with a content-driven launch cycle.
4. **Transparent background as a hard requirement** — They use their photo in Canva, Figma, and with designers regularly. They need their headshot on a transparent background so it can be placed on branded backgrounds. This is a concrete professional requirement, not a nice-to-have.
5. **Style experimentation before committing** — They want to see how they look in Speaker style vs. Creative Industry style before deciding which direction to take their brand. The ability to generate multiple variants is genuinely useful to them.

### Trigger Events

| Trigger | Description |
|---|---|
| New offer launch | Course, coaching program, or consulting tier launching. Photo is on the asset checklist. |
| Speaking engagement secured | Event organizer requests a "high-resolution speaker photo." Current photos are wrong style or too low-res. |
| Brand redesign | Designer redoing website or newsletter needs a photo that matches the new aesthetic. |
| Podcast or YouTube launch | Need a consistent, camera-ready look for thumbnails and channel art. |
| New market entry | Pivoting from B2B consulting to consumer coaching (or vice versa). Visual presence must signal the new positioning. |
| Peer comparison | Follows someone in their space who looks incredible across every platform. Feels the contrast acutely. |

### Feature Affinity

| Feature | Priority | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| Speaker style | Critical | Specific current use case. "Conference keynote presenter" framing is exactly what event organizers need. |
| Creative Industry style | Critical | Closest match to "independent expert authority" — not corporate, not casual. |
| Transparent background (Pro) | Critical | Hard requirement for design workflows. Non-negotiable. |
| 2048px export (Pro) | Critical | Website hero images, course platform headers, and conference print materials require high resolution. |
| Multiple style generation | High | Will generate Speaker + Creative Industry + LinkedIn to compare before committing. |
| Approachable Expert expression | High | Maps perfectly to creator/consultant brand positioning. |
| Regional editing — background | High | Will change background to match brand aesthetic (specific color, gradient, abstract studio). |
| Platform batch export (Download All) | High | Needs LinkedIn, Twitter, and website versions simultaneously. |
| Identity locks (skin tone, eye color) | High | Any AI tool that changes their skin tone or eye color is immediately disqualifying. |
| Naturalness — Natural preset (15) | High | Specifically does not want the over-smoothed AI look. Will set naturalness low to preserve texture. |
| Settings JSON copy | High | Will save optimal configuration; may share with VA or a community of practice. |
| Regional editing — clothes | Medium | Will adjust if AI generates corporate attire that conflicts with their brand positioning. |
| History strip + undo/redo | Medium | Power user who iterates. Edit history is meaningful quality-of-life. |
| Serious Authority expression | Medium | Alternate expression for B2B consulting proposals vs. consumer-facing content. |

**Not relevant**: Resume style, Corporate style (actively signals the wrong thing), Dating style.

### Willingness to Pay

**Free tier**: They will try free but will hit the Pro wall immediately — transparent background PNG and 2048px are required for their current workflow. They are not evaluating whether to pay; they are evaluating whether output quality is sufficient to pay for.

**Pro upgrade trigger**: Transparent background requirement alone is sufficient. At $9.99, they have already paid more for a single Canva template. The question is output quality, not cost.

**Estimated Pro conversion**: 55–70% of users who complete generation.

**Repeat use**: Every 6–12 months when brand aesthetic evolves, new offer launches, or major speaking engagement secured. Strong organic word-of-mouth — the "how did you get that photo?" conversation is natural in their professional communities.

**LTV driver**: Referrals. They are embedded in mastermind groups, Slack communities, and paid creator cohorts. A visible on-brand headshot update generates organic referral traffic from exactly the same ICP profile.

### Discovery Channels

1. Creator/freelancer Slack and Discord communities — Circles, Indie Hackers, Women in Tech, freelance Slack groups. Someone posts their result and "how did you get that?" generates traffic.
2. Newsletter recommendations — Creator-focused newsletters (Morning Brew Creator, solopreneur newsletters). Featured tool recommendations convert immediately.
3. Twitter/X creator accounts — Follows productivity and solopreneur accounts. Tool mentions from trusted creators carry high weight.
4. Product Hunt — Browses systematically; high conversion rate on discovered tools.
5. Google search — "AI headshot transparent background," "professional speaker photo AI," "brand photographer alternative."
6. Podcast mentions — Listens to solopreneur/creator podcasts. Tool mentions in interview context are high-conversion.

### Objections

| Objection | Response |
|---|---|
| "The output will look like AI and damage my brand." | Naturalness slider set low (15–25) for texture preservation + identity locks + Approachable Expert expression avoids dead-eye stare. Before/after slider validates before committing. |
| "I need exact background colors to match my brand." | Regional background presets (Solid White/Grey/Gradient/Abstract Studio/Transparent) + free-text custom instruction. Must be surfaced clearly in onboarding. |
| "It won't capture my brand's energy." | 16 style variants + 5 expression combinations give enough variation to find a match. Encourage generating Speaker + Creative Industry in parallel. |
| "My designer needs a specific file format." | Pro tier PNG export at 2048px. Direct technical answer to a technical objection. |
| "I don't want Google having my face." | More acute for this ICP — they are public-facing and privacy-aware. Privacy notice must clearly distinguish between ProPortrait (no server storage) and Google Gemini (API processing). |

### Success Metrics

They consider ProPortrait successful if:
- Their designer accepts the PNG export without needing edge cleanup or background removal adjustments
- The photo works at all aspect ratios (1:1 LinkedIn, 3:4 website) without looking awkwardly cropped
- At least one peer asks what photographer they used
- They can refresh their visual presence within 30 minutes when a new speaking bio is needed
- The transparent background PNG drops cleanly into design files without artifacts
- Their brand colors, photo aesthetic, and tone feel coherent together — the photo looks like it was shot for their current brand
